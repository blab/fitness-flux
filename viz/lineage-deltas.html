Lineage deltas

Along each SARS-CoV-2 parent→child lineage branch, how does the change in fitness relate to the mutations acquired and to mutation-effect predictors (CoVFit, EvEscape, Bloom-lab deep mutational scanning, and ESM protein-language-model embeddings)? A Python + Observable Plot port of lineage-deltas-analysis/lineage-deltas.nb.

Branch mutations vs. fitness change

Each point is one parent→child branch in one season: change in a chosen mutation region against change in log fitness. RBD substitutions carry the strongest positive signal; non-spike substitutions almost none.

Slope of the mutation–fitness relationship through time

Per-season regression slope of fitness change on mutation change. The fitness value of spike change is largest early and erodes as the population approaches the limits of antigenic escape.

Linear model across mutation regions

Coefficients of a multiple regression of fitness change on per-region mutation-count change, fit over all branches and split into early vs. late seasons. R² is shown per group.

Predictor correlations

Pearson correlation of each predictor's branch delta with the fitness change. Select a predictor to see its scatter.

Data files are fetched at runtime from ../lineage-deltas-analysis/results/. The Mathematica notebook is retained for reference.
