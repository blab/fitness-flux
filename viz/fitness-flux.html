Fitness flux

Variant fitness reconstructed across overlapping seasonal multinomial-logistic-regression (MLR) fits, stitched onto a common "scaffolded" scale, and used to characterize the traveling wave of fitness through time. This is a Python + Observable Plot port of fitness-flux-analysis/fitness-flux.nb.

Scaffolded fitness through time

Each variant's scaffolded log fitness plotted at its frequency-weighted mean date. Fitness climbs as the population explores successive adaptive variants.

Variant frequencies through time

Empirical weekly frequencies (renormalized across the retained variants). Only variants reaching an appreciable peak frequency are drawn.

Mutations and fitness

Receptor-binding-protein amino-acid substitution count versus scaffolded fitness (left) and versus mean date (right): fitness and mutational distance both accrue over time.

The fitness wave (flux)

The population mean log fitness is the centre of a traveling wave. Its rate of advance (velocity, the flux) tracks the variance in fitness across co-circulating variants — Fisher's fundamental theorem. The variance-vs-velocity slope is reported below.
